How to use the database

To generate a custom table displaying properties of transient source models, please select the source categories and the properties to be displayed, and then click on "Generate the table". You can also export a csv version of this table by clicking on "Export the table (csv)". This information has been extracted from existing transient source models, and the related papers are referenced in the table with their arXiv link. If you spot a mistake, please contact us.

Definition of model properties

Transient category Display the transient category as defined in the paper. Sub-categories can be used with respect to our definition of source categories.
Modeling Brief summary of the type of model adopted in the paper. Generally includes some information about the type of signals modeled, the type of interaction, the region in the source and some original aspects.
Motivation Specific observational or theoretical motivation.
Results Selected results, usually found in the abstract of the paper.
Perspectives Selected perspectives, interesting for further modeling.
Codes Simulation tools used for the model.
Zones Transient source models usually involve one or more zones inside the source, where different processes can take place. In one zone models particles are accelerated, propagate and interact in a region that produces all observed emission. When this type of description is inadequate, for instance, when one zone models fail to explain multi-wavelength or MM data, two zone models can be adopted. For example, acceleration can be treated prior to interactions (first zone) and accelerated hadrons and leptons can be injected in the interaction region (second zone) where they only propagate and interact. Another example is the use of an external radiation background as a target for interactions. The use of two-zone models should be well motivated by a sufficient amount of available multi-wavelength or MM data, due to the caveat that considering two or more zones allows for additional degrees of freedom.
Composition Cosmic-ray composition. Most models consider pure proton, some include nuclei.
Acceleration Phenomenological models describe an acceleration timescale and a spectrum for accelerated particles, often modeled by a power-law of maximum energy E_max and spectral index alpha. Various acceleration processes involve the scattering of charged particles on magnetic inhomogeneities. A precise description of acceleration processes, such as shock acceleration or magnetic reconnection for instance, requires the modeling of the astrophysical plasma and the complex interplay between the charged particles and the electromagnetic fields, at scales of the order of the plasma skin depth.
Interactions Primary and secondary interactions involved in the neutrino production.
Maximum energies Cosmic-ray and neutrino maximum energies after acceleration, energy losses and interactions.
Spectra Various spectra predicted by the model and their characteristics.

Lightcurves

 When time-dependences are accounted for, description of the method used to predict the neutrino luminosity as a function of time. For instance, the time evolution of the source can be separated in different epochs, or multi-wavelength data can be used to map the neutrino luminosity.
Uncertainties When uncertainties are accounted for, method used for parameter scan and/or fitting procedure.
